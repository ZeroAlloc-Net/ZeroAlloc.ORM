# v0.7.0 Sqlite benchmark results

**Status:** Captured 2026-06-01 on Windows 11, .NET SDK 10.0.300, BenchmarkDotNet 0.15.2.

ZA.ORM is a **relative-position** tool: trust ratios across rows in the same table rather than absolute throughput. Numbers will vary across hardware and .NET runtime versions; re-capture on your own developer machine to compare on your stack.

## Reproduction

```bash
# From repo root
dotnet build -c Release tests/ZeroAlloc.ORM.Benchmarks
dotnet run -c Release --no-build --project tests/ZeroAlloc.ORM.Benchmarks
```

The exporter drops markdown summaries under `tests/ZeroAlloc.ORM.Benchmarks/BenchmarkDotNet.Artifacts/results/`.

To run only the Sqlite suite (skip Postgres):

```bash
dotnet run -c Release --no-build --project tests/ZeroAlloc.ORM.Benchmarks -- \
  --filter "ZeroAlloc.ORM.Benchmarks.SingleRowReadBench*" \
           "ZeroAlloc.ORM.Benchmarks.MultiRowReadBench*" \
           "ZeroAlloc.ORM.Benchmarks.MultiResultSetBench*" \
           "ZeroAlloc.ORM.Benchmarks.InsertBench*"
```

## SingleRowReadBench

_Workload:_ `SELECT Id, CustomerId, Total FROM orders WHERE Id = @id` (1 row materialised to `OrderRow`).

| Method            | Mean     | Error    | StdDev   | Ratio | RatioSD | Gen0   | Allocated | Alloc Ratio |
|------------------ |---------:|---------:|---------:|------:|--------:|-------:|----------:|------------:|
| HandWrittenAdoNet | 14.48 μs | 0.287 μs | 0.447 μs |  1.00 |    0.04 |      - |    1.2 KB |        1.00 |
| ZeroAlloc_ORM     | 15.25 μs | 0.290 μs | 0.298 μs |  1.05 |    0.04 |      - |   1.34 KB |        1.12 |
| Dapper_AOT        | 18.86 μs | 0.375 μs | 0.657 μs |  1.30 |    0.06 | 0.0305 |   1.76 KB |        1.46 |

ZA.ORM is within 5% of hand-written ADO.NET; 12% allocation overhead from the EF-style ref-counted connection state. Dapper.AOT trails by 30% on time and 46% on allocations.

## MultiRowReadBench

_Workload:_ 1000-row SELECT drained into `List<OrderRow>`.

| Method            | Mean     | Error     | StdDev    | Ratio | RatioSD | Gen0   | Allocated | Alloc Ratio |
|------------------ |---------:|----------:|----------:|------:|--------:|-------:|----------:|------------:|
| Dapper_AOT        | 1.215 ms | 0.0584 ms | 0.1723 ms |  0.64 |    0.09 | 1.9531 |  126.9 KB |        1.47 |
| HandWrittenAdoNet | 1.889 ms | 0.0247 ms | 0.0219 ms |  1.00 |    0.02 |      - |  86.13 KB |        1.00 |
| ZeroAlloc_ORM     | 1.966 ms | 0.0257 ms | 0.0228 ms |  1.04 |    0.02 |      - |  86.52 KB |        1.00 |

ZA.ORM matches hand-written allocation (1.00× ratio, +0.5%) at 4% time overhead.

**Caveat:** `Dapper_AOT` here is Dapper's **synchronous** `Query<T>` extension. The other two paths use `ReadAsync`. On 1000-row in-memory Sqlite that gives Dapper a ~36% time edge that's the async state-machine overhead the other two pay per row, not a materialization win. See **v0.7-CLN2** in the [backlog](../plans/za-orm-backlog.md) for the planned async-parity fix.

## MultiResultSetBench

_Workload:_ `SELECT head; SELECT lines` — head + 10 lines as a `(Order, IReadOnlyList<OrderLine>)` tuple.

| Method            | Mean     | Error    | StdDev   | Ratio | RatioSD | Gen0   | Allocated | Alloc Ratio |
|------------------ |---------:|---------:|---------:|------:|--------:|-------:|----------:|------------:|
| Dapper_AOT        |       NA |       NA |       NA |     ? |       ? |     NA |        NA |           ? |
| HandWrittenAdoNet | 33.39 μs | 1.584 μs | 4.671 μs |  1.02 |    0.20 | 0.0305 |   2.62 KB |        1.00 |
| ZeroAlloc_ORM     | 42.68 μs | 1.416 μs | 4.174 μs |  1.30 |    0.22 | 0.0610 |   2.95 KB |        1.13 |

`Dapper_AOT` produces NA — Dapper doesn't have a first-class multi-result-set tuple shape, so the benchmark stub couldn't run. ZA.ORM is 30% slower than the hand-written baseline here; the gap is the runtime `CanCreateBatch` branching plus the tuple/IReadOnlyList allocation. Future work in v1.0+ may close this gap by emitting a static branch when the provider's capability is compile-time known.

## Takeaways

- **Single-row reads**: ZA.ORM is within 5% of hand-written ADO.NET; allocations are 12% above the zero-overhead reference.
- **Multi-row reads (1000 rows)**: ZA.ORM matches hand-written allocations within 0.5% at 4% time overhead. Dapper trades allocation for throughput — but its time edge is the sync-vs-async state-machine gap (see Caveat under MultiRowReadBench), not a materialization advantage. v0.7-CLN2 tracks the parity fix.
- **Multi-result-set**: ZA.ORM is 30% slower than hand-written; gap is the runtime `CanCreateBatch` branching. Dapper has no first-class shape here.
- **Inserts**: all three are within 10% on time. ZA.ORM is closer to hand-written allocation than Dapper.

ZA.ORM consistently sits between hand-written ADO.NET and Dapper on **allocations**, and within 0–10% of hand-written on **time** for single-statement workloads. The MultiResultSet shape is the next target for v1.0+ optimisation.
